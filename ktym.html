Kottayam – My Hometown

Cradled by hills, carved by history.

Kottayam, nestled in the heart of Kerala, is a town where tradition and nature exist in quiet harmony. Known for its rich cultural legacy, scenic landscapes, and educational achievements, it's a place that reflects the essence of Kerala itself. From the whispering rubber plantations to the serene backwaters, Kottayam offers more than just sights — it offers a sense of belonging.

The Land of Latex

Kottayam has long held the title of "The Land of Latex", thanks to its lush rubber plantations. As one of India’s largest producers of natural rubber, the district plays a vital role in Kerala's economy. The rhythmic tapping of rubber trees is part of daily life here, transforming this agricultural product into a source of local pride.

Kerala’s Literacy Heartbeat

Not just a town, but Kerala’s literacy heartbeat.

Kottayam was the first town in India to achieve 100% literacy. Known as Akshara Nagari or "City of Letters," it’s a hub of publishing, home to prominent newspapers like Malayala Manorama and Deepika. From educational pioneers to literary leaders, Kottayam continues to inspire minds and spread knowledge.

Culture and Nature in Harmony

The cottage between the backwaters and the blue hills.

Surrounded by the peaceful Vembanad Lake and lush green hills, Kottayam is blessed with both natural serenity and deep-rooted culture. From ancient churches and temples to scenic spots like Illickal Kallu, the district welcomes every visitor with quiet charm and timeless grace.

Did You Know?

Rubber Capital: Kottayam produces over 75% of Kerala’s natural rubber.
100% Literacy: First district in India to achieve complete literacy (1989).
City of Letters: Home to major newspapers like Malayala Manorama and Deepika.
Name Origin: ‘Kottayam’ comes from ‘Kotta’ (fort) and ‘Akam’ (inside).
Illickal Kallu: A scenic and misty hilltop offering breathtaking views.
